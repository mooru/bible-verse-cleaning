###### v1
a. now it happened when the crowd pressed against Him
b. indeed to hear the message of God
c. so He himself was standing by the lake of Gennesaret

###### v2
a. and He saw two boats being by the lake
b. further the fishermen having gone away from them
c. were washing the nets

###### v3
a. then having stepped into one of the boats
b. that was of Simon
c. He asked him
d. to put out (a) little away from the land
e. then having sat down
f. He taught the crowd from the boat

###### v4
a. now when He stopped speaking
b. He said to Simon
c. put out to sea to the deep water
d. and let down your nets for (the) catch

###### v5
a. then Simon having replied said
b. Master having worked hard through all (the) night
c. we caught nothing
d. but at Your word I will let down the nets

###### v6
a. then (they) having done this
b. they caught (a) very large number of fish
c. and their nets were ripping

###### v7
a. so they motioned to their partners in the other boat
b. to having come help them
c. and so they came
d. and they filled both the boats
e. so that they were sinking

###### v8
a. then having seen (it)
b. Simon Peter fell down at the knees of Jesus saying
c. go away from me
d. for I am a sinful man Lord

###### v9
a. because astonishment seized him
b. and also all those with him
c. because of the catch of fish that they took

###### v10
a. and likewise also James and John (the) sons of Zebedee
b. who were partners with Simon
c. and then Jesus said to Simon
d. do not be afraid
e. from now on you will be making people obey

###### v11
a. then having brought their boats to shore upon the land
b. having left everything behind
c. they followed Him

###### v12
a. now it happened when He was in one of the towns
b. indeed take note
c. (a) man full of leprosy
d. and having seen Jesus
e. having fallen down before (Him) on (his) face
f. he pleaded with Him saying
g. Lord if You would want
h. You can heal me

###### v13
a. then having stretched out His hand
b. He touched him saying
c. I am willing
d. be healed
e. then immediately the leprosy left from him

###### v14
a. further He himself commanded him
b. to speak to no one
c. but having gone show yourself to the priest
d. and present (the offering) for your purification
e. just as Moses commanded
f. as evidence to them

###### v15
a. then the report about Him spread even more
b. and large crowds came together to listen to (Him)
c. and to be healed from their illnesses

###### v16
a. now He himself was withdrawing into the lonely places
b. and praying

###### v17
a. and so it happened on one of the days
b. now He was teaching
c. and so Pharisees and teachers of the Law were sitting down
d. who were ones having come from every village of Galilee and of Judea
e. yes even (from) Jerusalem
f. and also (the) power of (the) Lord was upon Him to heal

###### v18
a. now pay attention
b. men bringing on (a) bed (a) man
c. who was paralysed
d. and then they tried to bring him in
e. and to place [him] in front of Him

###### v19
a. then not having found what (way)
b. they could bring him in because of the crowd
c. having gone up onto the housetop
d. they let him down through the tiles with his bed
e. into the midst in front of Jesus

###### v20
a. then having seen their faith
b. He said
c. friend your sins are forgiven you

###### v21
a. then the Law experts and the Pharisees began to discuss saying
b. who is This
c. Who speaks blasphemies?
d. who can forgive sins except only God?

###### v22
a. then Jesus knowing their reasonings
b. having answered He said to them
c. why are you reasoning in your minds?

###### v23
a. what is easier to say
b. your sins are forgiven you
c. or to say
d. get up and walk?

###### v24
a. but so that you may know that
b. the Son of Man has power
c. to forgive sins on the earth
d. He said to the paralysed man
e. I say to you
f. get up
g. and having picked up your bed
h. go to your home

###### v25
a. then immediately having stood up before them
b. having picked up on what he was lying
c. he went away to his home
d. glorifying God

###### v26
a. then amazement took hold of all
b. and they glorified God
c. and so they were filled with fear
d. saying that
e. we saw strange things today

###### v27
a. then after these things He went out
b. and He saw (a) tax collector by name Levi
c. sitting at the tax office
d. and He said to him
e. follow Me

###### v28
a. then having left behind everything
b. having stood up he followed Him

###### v29
a. now Levi held (a) great banquet for Him in his house
b. and there was (a) large crowd of tax collectors
c. and others who were eating with them

###### v30
a. then the Pharisees and their Law experts were complaining
b. saying to His disciples
c. why do you eat and drink with the tax collectors and sinners?

###### v31
a. then Jesus having replied said to them
b. those being healthy have no need of (a) doctor
c. certainly those being ill

###### v32
a. I have not come to call righteous people
b. but sinners unto repentance

###### v33
a. then they said to Him
b. the disciples of John fast frequently
c. and they do prayers
d. just like also those (disciples) of the Pharisees
e. but those with You eat and drink

###### v34
a. then Jesus said to them
b. surely you can not the wedding guests
c. as long as the bridegroom is with them
d. make (them) to fast?

###### v35
a. but days will come
b. when indeed the bridegroom is taken away from them
c. then they will fast on those days

###### v36
a. then also He told (a) parable to them that
b. no one having torn (a) patch from (a) new garment puts (it)
c. on (an) old garment
d. and if not indeed
e. also the new one will tear
f. indeed  the patch from the new does not match the old

###### v37
a. and no one puts new wine into old wineskins
b. and if not indeed
c. the new wine will burst the wineskins
d. and (the wine) self will be spilled
e. and the wineskins will be ruined

###### v38
a. but new wine must be put into new wineskins

###### v39
a. [further] no one having drunk old (wine)
b. wants new (wine)
c. because he says
d. the old is good
