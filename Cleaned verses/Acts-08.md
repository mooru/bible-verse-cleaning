###### v2
a. then devout men buried Stephen
b. and they made great lamentation over him

###### v3
a. further Saul was causing great harm to the church
b. entering from house to house
c. dragging men as well as women
d. he handed (them) over to prison

###### v4
a. then indeed they having been scattered
b. they went on preaching the message

###### v5
a. further Philip having gone down to [the] town of Samaria
b. he proclaimed to them the Messiah

###### v6
a. and the crowd paid close attention to that being said by Philip with one mind
b. while they hear and see the signs that he was doing

###### v7
a. indeed many of those having unclean spirits
b. shouting with (a) loud voice they were going out
c. and many paralysed and lame were healed

###### v8
a. so there was much joy in that town

###### v9
a. but a certain man by name Simon
b. was formerly in the town practising magic
c. and greatly astounding the people of Samaria
d. saying that he himself was very important

###### v10
a. to whom all paid close attention from (the) least to (the) important saying
b. this one is the power of God that is called great

###### v11
a. and they paid close attention to him
b. because for (a) long time he greatly astounded them with magic

###### v12
a. but when they believed Philip
b. telling the good news about the Kingdom of God
c. and the Name of Jesus Christ
d. men as well as women were being baptised

###### v13
a. and even Simon himself believed
b. and having been baptised he was staying close to Philip
c. seeing signs as well as great miracles happening he was greatly astonished

###### v14
a. then the apostles in Jerusalem having heard that
b. Samaria has received the message of God
c. they sent to them Peter and John

###### v15
a. who having gone down prayed for them
b. that they may receive (the) Holy Spirit

###### v16
a. for He had not yet come upon no one of them
b. but they were only baptised in the Name of the Lord Jesus

###### v17
a. then they placed their hands on them
b. and they received (the) Holy Spirit

###### v18
a. now Simon having seen that
b. through the laying on of the hands of the apostles
c. the Spirit is given

###### v19
a. he presented to them money saying
b. give me also this power
c. so that on who ever I place my hands
d. may receive (the) Holy Spirit

###### v20
a. but Peter said to him
b. may your money be with you unto destruction
c. because you thought to obtain the gift of God with money

###### v21
a. there is not (a) part and not (a) share for you in this matter
b. because your inner self is not right before God

###### v22
a. repent therefore from this wickedness of you
b. and plead with the Lord
c. if perhaps the intention of your inner self will be forgiven for you

###### v23
a. because I see you being terribly envious and (in the) bond of wickedness

###### v24
a. then having answered Simon said
b. plead you on my behalf with the Lord
c. so that nothing may happen to me of what you have said

###### v25
a. they now then having testified
b. and having spoken the message of the Lord
c. they returned to Jerusalem
d. and (to) many villages of the Samaritans they told the good news

###### v26
a. further (an) angel of (the) Lord spoke to Philip saying
b. stand up and go down to (the) south
c. on the road the one going down from Jerusalem to Gaza
d. this (road) is deserted

###### v27
a. then having got up he went
b. now pay attention
c. (a) man an Ethiopian eunuch
d. (an) official of Candace queen of (the) Ethiopians
e. who was responsible for all her treasury
f. who had come worshipping in Jerusalem

###### v28
a. and he was returning and sitting on his chariot
b. and he was reading the prophet Isaiah

###### v29
a. then the Spirit said to Philip
b. go forward and be joined to this chariot

###### v30
a. so Philip having run up to (the chariot)
b. heard him reading Isaiah the prophet
c. then he said
d. do you then indeed understand what you read?

###### v31
a. but he said
b. how in fact would I be able
c. if someone will not lead me to know?
d. and he invited Philip having climbed up to sit with him

###### v32
a. now the passage of the Scripture that he was reading was this
b. like (a) sheep He was led for slaughter
c. and without a sound like (a) lamb before the one shearing him
d. so He does not open His mouth

###### v33
a. in [His] humility His justice was taken away
b. who will tell (about) His offspring?
c. because from the earth His life is taken away

###### v34
a. then having answered the eunuch said to Philip
b. I beg you about whom does the prophet say this?
c. about himself or about someone else?

###### v35
a. so Philip having opened his mouth
b. then having begun from this passage
c. he told him the good news about Jesus

###### v36
a. then while they were going along the road
b. they came to some water
c. and the eunuch said
d. look water
e. what prevents me to be baptised?

###### v38
a. then he ordered the chariot to stop
b. and they both went down into the water
c. Philip as well as the eunuch
d. and he baptised him

###### v39
a. then when they went up out of the water
b. (the) Spirit of (the) Lord took Philip away
c. and the eunuch saw him not any longer
d. indeed he went along his way rejoicing

###### v40
a. then Philip was found in Azotus
b. and travelling through he told the good news to all the towns
c. until he came to Caesarea
