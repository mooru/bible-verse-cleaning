###### v1
a. then it happened in Iconium according to the same that they went into the synagogue of the Jews
b. and spoke in this way
c. so that (a) big group of Jews as well as Greeks believed

###### v2
a. but those Jews refusing to believe stirred up
b. and turned the Gentiles against the fellow believers

###### v3
a. and so then they stayed for (a) long time
b. speaking boldly about the Lord
c. the One witnessing [to] the message of His grace
d. by causing signs and wonders to happen through their power

###### v4
a. but the people of the city were divided
b. now some then were with the Jews
c. and others with the apostles

###### v5
a. but when there was a desire of the Gentiles
b. as well as of Jews together with their rulers
c. to maltreat and stone them

###### v6
a. having found out about (it)
b. they fled to the cities of Lycaonia Lystra and Derbe
c. and the surrounding region

###### v7
a. and they were there telling the good news

###### v8
a. then (a) certain man in Lystra incapable with regard to his feet was sitting
b. lame since his mother's womb
c. who never walked

###### v9
a. this one heard Paul speaking
b. who having looked straight at him
c. and having seen that
d. he has faith to be healed

###### v10
a. he said with (a) loud voice
b. stand up straight on your feet
c. then he jumped up
d. and he was walking

###### v11
a. and then the crowds having seen what Paul did
b. raised their voices speaking in Lycaonian
c. the gods having resembled people came down to us

###### v12
a. thus they were calling Barnabas Zeus
b. and Paul Hermes
c. since he was the one leading in the speaking

###### v13
a. and then the priest
b. of Zeus the one being in front of the city
c. having brought bulls and wreaths to the gates
d. he wanted to make a sacrifice together with the crowds

###### v14
a. but the apostles Barnabas and Paul having heard (this)
b. having torn their clothes
c. they rushed out into the crowd shouting

###### v15
a. and saying
b. men why are you doing these things?
c. we too are human beings (having the) same kind of feelings with you
d. bringing you the good news
e. to turn away from these futile things to (the) living God
f. Who made the heaven and the earth
g. and the sea and all things in them

###### v16
a. Who during past generations
b. allowed all the nations to go their ways

###### v17
a. even though He did not leave himself without witness
b. doing good
c. giving you from the sky rains and fruitful periods of time
d. filling your inner selves with food and joyfulness

###### v18
a. now saying these things
b. they with difficulty stopped the crowds not to sacrifice to them

###### v19
a. then Jews arrived from Antioch and Iconium
b. and having persuaded the crowds
c. and having stoned Paul
d. they dragged (him) out of the city
e. thinking that he was dead

###### v20
a. but the disciples having surrounded him
b. he having stood up
c. he went into the city

###### v21
a. then having brought the good news (to) that city
b. and having made many disciples
c. they returned to Lystra and to Iconium and to Antioch

###### v22
a. strengthening the inner selves of the disciples
b. encouraging (them) to continue in the faith
c. and that through many sufferings
d. we must enter into the Kingdom of God

###### v23
a. then having appointed for them elders from church to church
b. having prayed with fastings
c. they entrusted them to the Lord
d. in Whom they had believed

###### v24
a. and having gone through Pisidia
b. they came to Pamphylia

###### v25
a. and having spoken the message in Perga
b. they went down to Attalia

###### v26
a. and from there they sailed away to Antioch
b. from where they were committed to the grace of God
c. for the work which they completed

###### v27
a. then having arrived
b. and having gathered the church together
c. they were reporting
d. how much God did through them
e. and that He opened for the Gentiles (a) door of faith

###### v28
a. and then they were staying not for a short time with the disciples
