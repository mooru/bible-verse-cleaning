###### v1
a. let thus (a) person regard us as servants of Christ
b. and administrators of (the) mysteries of God

###### v2
a. in this case furthermore it is desired among administrators that
b. anyone should be found trustworthy

###### v3
a. but with me it is in the end of very little importance that
b. I should be judged carefully by you or by (a) human court of justice
c. surely I judge not even myself carefully

###### v4
a. because I am aware of nothing as to myself
b. but I have not been acquitted by this
c. now then the One judging me carefully is (the) Lord

###### v5
a. so that you do not judge anything before (the) time
b. until the Lord would come
c. Who indeed will make known the hidden things of the darkness
d. and He will make visible the intentions of the inner selves
e. and then the praise will come to each from God

###### v6
a. now these things fellow believers I applied to myself and Apollos for your sake
b. so that you may learn from us not above what is written
c. so that not one may be made proud on behalf of one against the other

###### v7
a. because who evaluates you carefully?
b. and what do you have that you did not receive?
c. now since indeed you received
d. why do you boast like not having received?

###### v8
a. already you are having enough
b. already you became rich
c. without us you ruled
d. further I would that indeed you ruled
e. so that also we may rule with you

###### v9
a. because I presume
b. God showed us publicly (to be) the least important apostles
c. like ones sentenced to die
d. because we became (a) spectacle to the world
e. indeed to angels and to people

###### v10
a. we (are) foolish for the sake of Christ
b. but you (are) wise with regard to Christ
c. we (are) weak
d. but you (are) powerful
e. you (are) honoured
f. but we (are) dishonoured

###### v11
a. until this very time we are both hungry and thirsty
b. and we are wearing rags and are caused harm and are homeless

###### v12
a. indeed we labour working with our own hands
b. being slandered we act kindly
c. being persecuted we have patience

###### v13
a. being defamed we encourage
b. we became like rubbish of the world
c. rubbish of all things until now

###### v14
a. I write these things not making you ashamed
b. but like admonishing my beloved children

###### v15
a. for if you may have countless guardians with regard to Christ
b. certainly (you do) not (have) many fathers
c. for in Christ Jesus I became your father through the gospel

###### v16
a. so then I ask you earnestly
b. become my imitators

###### v17
a. because of this I sent Timothy to you
b. who is my beloved son and trustworthy in (the) Lord
c. who will cause you to remember my way of life in Christ [Jesus]
d. just as I teach everywhere in every church

###### v18
a. now because of my not coming to you
b. some were made arrogant

###### v19
a. but I will soon come to you
b. if the Lord would want
c. and I will find out not the speech of the ones having been made  
d. but their power

###### v20
a. because the Kingdom of God (is) not by word
b. but by power

###### v21
a. what do you desire?
b. should I come to you with (a) rod
c. or with love and (an) attitude of gentleness?
