###### v1
a. now all the tax collectors and the sinners were drawing near to Him to listen to Him

###### v2
a. then both the Pharisees and the Law experts grumbled saying that
b. this Man receives sinners
c. He even eats with them

###### v3
a. then He spoke to them this parable saying

###### v4
a. a certain man among you having hundred sheep
b. then having lost one of them
c. does he not leave the ninety nine in the wilderness
d. and go after the lost one
e. until he would find it?

###### v5
a. and having found he places (it) on his shoulders rejoicing

###### v6
a. then having come into his house
b. he calls together his friends and his neighbours saying to them
c. rejoice with me
d. because I found my lost sheep

###### v7
a. I say to you that
b. there will be more joy in heaven
c. about one sinner repenting
d. than about ninety nine righteous ones who do not have need of repentance

###### v8
a. or a certain woman having ten coins
b. when she would lose one coin
c. does she not indeed light (a) lamp
d. and then sweeps the house
e. and seeks carefully until she finds? (it)

###### v9
a. then having found (it)
b. she calls together her friends and neighbours saying
c. rejoice with me
d. because I found the coin that I lost

###### v10
a. likewise I say to you
b. there is joy in the sight of the angels of God
c. because of one sinner repenting

###### v11
a. He said further
b. a certain man had two sons

###### v12
a. now the younger of them said to the father
b. father give me the share of the property belonging to (me)
c. he then divided the property between them

###### v13
a. now after not many days the younger son having turned everything into cash
b. he left home on a journey to (a) far land
c. and there he wasted his wealth living recklessly

###### v14
a. then having wasted all his things
b. (a) severe famine happened throughout that land
c. and he began to be in need

###### v15
a. then having gone he joined himself to one of the citizens of that country
b. in fact he sent him into his fields to look after (his) pigs

###### v16
a. and he desired greatly to be satisfied by the carob pods that the pigs ate
b. yet no one was giving (it) to him

###### v17
a. now having come to himself he said
b. how many hired workers of my father have plenty of food
c. but here I die of hunger

###### v18
a. having got up I will go to my father
b. and I will say to him
c. father I sinned against heaven and before you

###### v19
a. I am no longer worthy to be called your son
b. make me like one of your hired workers

###### v20
a. then having stood up he went to his own father
b. now while he is still far away
c. his father saw him
d. and he felt compassion for (him)
e. then having run
f. he embraced him
g. and he kissed him

###### v21
a. then the son said to him
b. father I sinned against heaven and before you
c. I am no longer worthy to be called your son

###### v22
a. then the father said to his slaves
b. quickly bring out the best long robe
c. and put (it) on him
d. and put (a) ring on his finger
e. and shoes on his feet

###### v23
a. and bring the grain-fattened calf
b. slaughter (it)
c. then having eaten let us celebrate

###### v24
a. because this son of mine was dead
b. and he came back to life
c. he was lost
d. and he was found
e. then they began to celebrate

###### v25
a. now his son the older one was in (the) field
b. and so while arriving he drew near to the house
c. he heard music and dancing

###### v26
a. then having called to himself one of the slaves
b. he enquired what this would be

###### v27
a. then he said to him that
b. your brother is here
c. and your father slaughtered the grain-fattened calf
d. because he received him back healthy

###### v28
a. then he was furious
b. and he did not want to come in
c. but his father having gone out invited him

###### v29
a. but he having replied said to his father
b. pay attention
c. so many years I serve you
d. and I never disobeyed your command
e. yet to me you never gave (a) goat
f. so that I may celebrate with my friends

###### v30
a. but when this son of yours the one having wasted your possessions with prostitutes came
b. you slaughtered the grain-fattened calf for him

###### v31
a. then he said to him
b. child you are always with me
c. and everything of mine is yours

###### v32
a. but it was necessary to celebrate and to rejoice
b. because this brother of yours was dead
c. and he came back to life
d. indeed he was lost
e. and he was found
