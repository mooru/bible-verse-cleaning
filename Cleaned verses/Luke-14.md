###### v1
a. now it happened when He came into (the) house of one of the leaders [of the] Pharisees
b. to eat food on (the) Sabbath
c. and so they were watching Him closely

###### v2
a. now pay attention
b. a certain man suffering from dropsy was in front of Him

###### v3
a. then Jesus having replied spoke to the Law experts and Pharisees saying
b. is it allowed on the Sabbath to heal or not?

###### v4
a. but they kept silent
b. then having taken hold of (the man) He healed him
c. and then He let (him) go

###### v5
a. further He said to them
b. (a) son of one of you or (an) ox will fall into (a) well
c. now will he not immediately pull him up on the Sabbath day?

###### v6
a. then they were not able to reply to this

###### v7
a. then He spoke (a) parable to those having been invited
b. watching how they chose the seats of honour
c. saying to them

###### v8
a. when you were invited by someone to (a) wedding
b. you should not sit down to eat on the seat of honour
c. so that there may not be one more honoured than you
d. having been invited by him

###### v9
a. then the one having invited you and him having come will say to you
b. give (the) place to this one
c. and then you would begin with shame to occupy the lowest place

###### v10
a. but when you were invited
b. having gone sit down to eat on the least important place
c. so that when the one having invited you comes he will say to you
d. friend move up higher
e. then there will be honour to you before all those eating together with you

###### v11
a. because everyone exalting himself
b. will be humbled
c. and the one humbling himself will be exalted

###### v12
a. then He said also to the one having invited him
b. when you give (a) lunch or (a) dinner
c. do not invite your friends nor your brothers nor your relatives nor (your) rich neighbours
d. indeed never those who would invite you back
e. so that it may become (a) repayment for you

###### v13
a. but when you hold (a) banquet
b. invite (the) poor (the) crippled (the) lame (the) blind

###### v14
a. indeed you will be happy
b. because they do not have to pay you back
c. for it will be paid back to you at the resurrection of the righteous

###### v15
a. then one of those reclining having heard said this to Him
b. happy (is the one) who will eat bread in the Kingdom of God

###### v16
a. but He said to him
b. a certain person gave (a) big banquet
c. and he invited many

###### v17
a. then he sent his slave at the hour of the banquet
b. to say to those having been invited
c. come
d. because it is already prepared

###### v18
a. then they all began one by one to refuse to accept
b. the first one said to him
c. I bought land
d. and I have (an) obligation having gone out to see it
e. I ask you
f. let me refuse to accept

###### v19
a. and another said
b. I bought five pair of oxen
c. and I am going to test them
d. I ask you
e. let me refuse to accept

###### v20
a. then another said
b. I married (a) woman
c. and because of this I can not come

###### v21
a. then the slave having come
b. he reported these things to his master
c. then the master of the household having become very angry
d. said to his slave
e. go out quickly into the wide streets and (the) narrow streets of the town
f. and bring in here the poor and (the) crippled and (the) blind and (the) lame

###### v22
a. then the slave said
b. master it has happened what you commanded
c. yet there is still space

###### v23
a. then the master said to the slave
b. go out into the roads and byways
c. and force (them) to come in
d. so that my house may be filled

###### v24
a. for I say to you that
b. no one of those men having been invited
c. will taste my banquet

###### v25
a. now then large crowds went with Him
b. and so having turned around He said to them

###### v26
a. if someone comes to Me
b. and he does not hate his own father and mother
c. and his wife and children
d. and his brothers and sisters
e. and even also his own life
f. he can not be My disciple

###### v27
a. he who does not carry his own cross
b. and comes after Me
c. he can not be My disciple

###### v28
a. for who among you wanting to build (a) tower
b. does not indeed first having sat down calculate the cost
c. whether he is able up to completion?

###### v29
a. so that if perhaps he having laid (a) foundation
b. and then he not being capable to complete
c. all those watching
d. would begin to mock him

###### v30
a. saying that
b. this man began to build
c. yet he was not capable to complete

###### v31
a. or what king going to meet another king for war
b. will not indeed first having sat down think carefully
c. if he is able to meet in battle with ten thousand
d. the one coming against him with twenty thousand?

###### v32
a. but if not indeed he still being far away
b. having sent (a) representative
c. he asks the things for peace

###### v33
a. so then everyone from you who does not part with all his possessions
b. he can not be My disciple

###### v34
a. indeed salt (is) good
b. but further when salt loses its taste
c. with what will it be made salty?

###### v35
a. neither for (the) soil nor for (the) dung heap is it suitable
b. they throw it outside
c. the one having ears to hear let him hear
