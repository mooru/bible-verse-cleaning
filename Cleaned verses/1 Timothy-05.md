###### v1
a. do not rebuke (an) old man
b. but appeal to (him) as (a) father
c. younger men as brothers

###### v2
a. older women as mothers
b. younger women as sisters
c. with all moral purity

###### v3
a. support and honour widows
b. the ones (being) really widows

###### v4
a. but if a certain widow has children or grandchildren
b. let them understand first
c. to fulfill their duties to their own family
d. and to make repayments to their ancestors
e. for this is pleasing before God

###### v5
a. now the one really (being a) widow
b. and also being without relatives
c. she has put her hope in God
d. and she persists in
e. pleas
f. and prayers
g. night and day

###### v6
a. but the one living indulgently
b. she has died living

###### v7
a. now command these things
b. so that they may be above criticism

###### v8
a. but if anyone of their own people
b. and especially of the relatives
c. does not take care
d. he has disregarded the faith
e. and is worse than (an) unbeliever

###### v9
a. let (a) widow be put on a list
b. not being less than sixty years
c. (the) wife of one man

###### v10
a. being spoken well of as to good works
b. that she raised children
c. that she showed hospitality
d. that she washed the feet of devout ones
e. that she helped ones suffering hardship
f. that she devoted herself to every good deed

###### v11
a. but avoid younger widows
b. for when they become passionate
c. away from Christ
d. they wish to marry

###### v12
a. causing condemnation
b. because they rejected their former promise

###### v13
a. further in addition also they learn (to be) idle
b. going around the houses
c. now (they are) not only idle
d. but also gossipy
e. and busybodies
f. speaking the things (that) should not

###### v14
a. so then I want younger ones to marry
b. to bear children
c. to direct a household
d. to give no opportunity to the one being hostile
e. for slander

###### v15
a. for already some went astray after Satan

###### v16
a. if any reliable woman has widows
b. let she provide for them
c. and let the church not be troubled
d. so that it can provide for those really widows

###### v17
a. the elders leading well
b. should be regarded as worthy of double honour
c. especially those labouring in preaching and teaching

###### v18
a. for the Scripture says
b. you shall not muzzle (an) ox treading out grain
c. and
d. the worker (is) worthy of his wage

###### v19
a. do not accept (an) accusation against (an) elder
b. except however by two or three witnesses

###### v20
a. those committing sin
b. rebuke (them) before all
c. so that also the others may have respect

###### v21
a. I warn (you) before God and Christ Jesus and the chosen  
b. that you keep these commandments
c. without partiality
d. doing nothing according to prejudice

###### v22
a. lay hands on no one quickly
b. and do not join in other people's sins
c. keep yourself pure

###### v23
a. drink no longer water (only)
b. but use (a) little wine
c. for the sake of your stomach
d. and your frequent illnesses

###### v24
a. the sins of some persons are very obvious
b. going ahead of (them) to condemnation
c. but for some also they accompany (them)

###### v25
a. likewise also the good works (are) very obvious
b. and those being different
c. can not be hidden
