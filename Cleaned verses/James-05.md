a. your riches have rotted away
b. and your garments have become moth-eaten

###### v3
a. your gold and silver have become tarnished
b. and their tarnish will be as (a) witness against you
c. and will consume your flesh like fire
d. you treasured up for (the) last days

###### v4
a. indeed the pay of the workers that mowed your fields
b. the (pay) defrauded by you
c. cries out
d. and the cries of the harvesters have entered into the ears of (the)  

###### v5
a. you lived on the earth with intemperance and indulgence
b. you fed your insides for (the) day of condemnation

###### v6
a. you condemned (the righteous)
b. you murdered the righteous
c. he shows you no hostility

###### v7
a. be patient then fellow believers
b. until the coming of the Lord
c. pay attention
d. the farmer waits for the valuable harvest of the soil
e. waiting patiently for it
f. until he received early rain and late rain

###### v8
a. you too must wait patiently
b. strengthen your inner selves
c. because the coming of the Lord is near

###### v9
a. do not complain against one another fellow believers
b. so that you may not be judged
c. pay attention
d. the judge has taken stand before the doors

###### v10
a. take (as) example fellow believers
b. of suffering distress and of patience the prophets
c. who spoke in the Name of (the) Lord

###### v11
a. pay attention
b. we regard as happy those having endured
c. you heard about the endurance of Job
d. and you noticed the purpose of (the) Lord
e. that the Lord is very compassionate and merciful

###### v12
a. now above all my fellow believers
b. do not swear
c. not by heaven
d. and not by the earth
e. and not by any other oath
f. but let your yes be yes
g. and (your) no no
h. so that you may not be condemned

###### v13
a. does anyone among you suffer distress?
b. let him pray
c. is anyone encouraged?
d. let him sing praises

###### v14
a. is anyone among you ill?
b. let him call to him the elders of the church
c. and let them pray for him
d. having anointed [him] with oil in the Name of the Lord

###### v15
a. and the prayer of faith will heal the sick
b. yes the Lord will restore him to health
c. even if he be one having committed sins
d. it will be forgiven to him

###### v16
a. confess then to one another your sins
b. indeed pray for one another
c. so that you may be healed
d. (the) prayer at work of (a) righteous person (has) great ability

###### v17
a. Elijah was (a) human being
b. similar to us in feeling
c. and by (a) prayer he prayed not to rain
d. and it did not rain on the earth for three years and six months

###### v18
a. then again he prayed
b. and the sky produced rain
c. and then the ground produced its fruit

###### v19
a. my fellow believers
b. if someone among you strayed from the truth
c. and someone made him change (his) beliefs

###### v20
a. let him know that
b. the one making (a) sinner change (his) beliefs
c. away from his deceitful way of life
d. will save his inner being from death
e. and he will cover a multitude of sins
