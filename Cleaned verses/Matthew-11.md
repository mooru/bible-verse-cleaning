###### v2
a. now John having heard in the prison about the deeds of Christ
b. having sent word through his disciples

###### v3
a. he said to Him
b. are You the One coming
c. or should we expect another?

###### v4
a. then Jesus having replied said to them
b. having gone report to John what you hear and see

###### v5
a. blind ones see again
b. and lame ones walk
c. lepers are healed and made ritually pure
d. and deaf ones are able to hear
e. and dead ones are raised to life
f. and poor people are told the good news

###### v6
a. indeed happy is who ever did not take offense in Me

###### v7
a. now while these people were leaving
b. Jesus began to speak to the crowd about John
c. what did you go out into the desert to see?
d. (a) reed being shaken by (the) wind?

###### v8
a. but what did you go out to see?
b. (a) man dressed in luxurious? (clothes)
c. look
d. the ones wearing luxurious (clothes)
e. they are in the houses of the kings

###### v9
a. but what did you go out to see?
b. (a) prophet?
c. yes I tell you
d. indeed much more than (a) prophet

###### v10
a. this one is about whom it is written
b. look
c. I send My messenger ahead of You
d. who will ahead of You make ready for You

###### v11
a. truly I say to you
b. among those born of women not one was raised up greater than John the Baptiser
c. but the more unimportant in the Kingdom of Heaven is greater than he

###### v12
a. but since the days of John the Baptiser until now
b. the Kingdom of Heaven suffers violent attacks
c. indeed violent people attack it

###### v13
a. indeed all the Prophets and the Law prophesied up to John

###### v14
a. and if you want to accept (it)
b. he is Elijah the one about to come

###### v15
a. the one able to hear let him listen

###### v16
a. but to what shall I compare this generation?
b. it is like children sitting in the marketplaces
c. who are calling out to the others

###### v17
a. they say
b. we played the flute for you
c. yet you did not dance
d. we sang funeral songs
e. yet you did not mourn

###### v18
a. then John came
b. not eating
c. and not drinking
d. and they say
e. he has (a) demon

###### v19
a. the Son of Man came
b. eating and drinking
c. and they say
d. look
e. (a) Man (who is a) glutton and drunkard
f. (a) friend of tax collectors and sinners
g. indeed wisdom was proved right by her deeds

###### v20
a. then He began to reprimand the towns
b. in which happened most miracles of Him
c. because they did not repent

###### v21
a. horror to you Chorazin
b. horror to you Bethsaida
c. because if in Tyre and Sidon the mighty deeds happened
d. the ones having occured in you
e. long ago they would have repented in sackcloth and ashes

###### v22
a. nevertheless I say to you
b. for Tyre and Sidon it will be more bearable on (the) day of judgment than to you

###### v23
a. and you Capernaum
b. do you not want to be lifted up as far as heaven?
c. you will go down as far as hell
d. because if in Sodom the mighty deeds happened
e. the ones having happened in you
f. it would have remained until today

###### v24
a. nevertheless I say to you that
b. for (the) land of Sodom it will be more bearable in (the) day of judgment than for you

###### v25
a. at that time Jesus having replied said
b. I thank You Father Lord of the heaven and the earth
c. that You hid these things from wise ones and intelligent ones
d. indeed You revealed them to small children

###### v26
a. yes Father
b. because in this way happened what is pleasing in your view

###### v27
a. everything was committed to Me by my Father
b. and no one knows the Son except the Father
c. neither does anyone know the Father except the Son
d. and to whom ever the Son intends to reveal (Him)

###### v28
a. come to Me all those being tired and being heavy laden
b. and I will give you rest

###### v29
a. take up My yoke upon you
b. and learn from Me that
c. I am gentle and humble at heart
d. and then you will find relief for your inner selves

###### v30
a. because My yoke (is) easy
b. and My burden is light
