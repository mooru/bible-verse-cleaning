###### v1
a. then some having come down from Judea
b. were teaching the fellow believers that
c. if you are not circumcised according to the custom of Moses
d. you can not be saved

###### v2
a. then not (a) small quarrel and dispute having taken place
b. by Paul and Barnabas against them
c. they appointed Paul and Barnabas and some others from them to go up
d. to the apostles and elders in Jerusalem
e. about this dispute

###### v3
a. they then indeed having been sent on their way by the church
b. they were going through Phoenicia as well as Samaria
c. telling about the turning to God of the Gentiles
d. and they were causing great joy to all the fellow believers

###### v4
a. so then having arrived in Jerusalem
b. they were welcomed by the church and the apostles and the elders
c. and they told how much God did through them

###### v5
a. but some of those from the religious party of the Pharisees stood up
b. they having been believers saying that
c. it is necessary to circumcise them
d. and to order (them) to keep the Law of Moses

###### v6
a. the apostles as well as the elders were gathered together
b. to consider about this matter

###### v7
a. then (a) great dispute having taken place
b. Peter having stood up
c. he said to them
d. men fellow believers you know that
e. long ago God chose among you
f. through my mouth the Gentiles to have heard the message of the gospel
g. and to have believed

###### v8
a. and God the One who knows what people think approved of them
b. giving (them) the Holy Spirit just as also to us

###### v9
a. and He not at all distinguished between both us and them as to the faith
b. having purified their inner selves

###### v10
a. now then why do you put God to the test
b. to place a burden on the backs of the disciples
c. which neither our ancestors nor we were able to endure?

###### v11
a. but through the grace of the Lord Jesus we believe to be saved
b. just like which manner also they

###### v12
a. then the whole crowd kept quiet
b. and they were listening to Barnabas and Paul reporting
c. what signs and wonders God did among the Gentiles through them

###### v13
a. and after they were silent
b. James spoke saying
c. men fellow believers listen to me

###### v14
a. Simeon told fully
b. how God first took care
c. to take from (the) nations (a) people for His Name

###### v15
a. and the messages of the prophets agree with this
b. just as it is written

###### v16
a. after these things I will return
b. and I will rebuild the tent of David the one having been destroyed
c. and I will restore its destruction
d. and so I will build it up again

###### v17
a. so that the rest of the people would seek the Lord diligently
b. indeed all the Gentiles upon whom My Name has been called upon them
c. says (the) Lord

###### v18
a. making these things well known long ago

###### v19
a. therefore I hold a view
b. not to cause extra difficulty for those from the Gentiles turning to God

###### v20
a. but to write to them a letter to abstain from
b. things defiled by idols
c. and (from) sexual immorality
d. and (from) what (is) strangled
e. and (from) blood

###### v21
a. because the Law of Moses has from ancient generations from city to city those preaching it
b. being read from every Sabbath to every Sabbath in the synagogues

###### v22
a. then it seemed to the apostles and the elders
b. with the whole church
c. having chosen men from them
d. to send to Antioch with Paul and Barnabas
e. Judas the one called Barsabbas and Silas
f. leading men among the fellow believers

###### v23
a. having written by their hand
b. the apostels and the elders (your) fellow believers
c. to the fellow believers throughout Antioch and Syria and Cilicia
d. to those from the Gentiles
e. greetings

###### v24
a. because we heard that
b. some from us [having gone out]
c. distressed you with words
d. upsetting your minds
e. to whom we did not give instructions

###### v25
a. it seemed to us having become with one mind
b. having chosen men
c. to send (them) to you
d. with our beloved Barnabas and Paul

###### v26
a. men having risked their lives
b. for the sake of the Name of our Lord Jesus Christ

###### v27
a. we have therefore sent Judas and Silas
b. they indeed telling through words the same things

###### v28
a. for it seemed to the Holy Spirit and to us
b. to subject you to no greater burden except these necessary things

###### v29
a. to abstain from meat of animals sacrificed to an idol
b. and (from) blood
c. and (from) strangled (animals)
d. and (from) sexual immorality
e. from which keeping yourselves from doing
f. you will do well
g. with best wishes

###### v30
a. they then indeed having been sent off
b. they went down to Antioch
c. and having gathered together the group
d. they delivered the letter

###### v31
a. then having read (it) they rejoiced because of the encouragement

###### v32
a. Judas as well as Silas also being prophets themselves
b. encouraged the fellow believers by much speaking
c. and so they strengthened (them)

###### v33
a. then having spent time
b. they were sent off with peace by the fellow believers
c. to the ones having sent them

###### v35
a. but Paul and Barnabas remained in Antioch
b. teaching and proclaiming the good news
c. with indeed many others the message of the Lord

###### v36
a. then after some days Paul said to Barnabas
b. having returned then
c. let us visit the fellow believers from every city to every city
d. in which we proclaimed the message of the Lord
e. (to see) how they are

###### v37
a. now Barnabas wanted to take along also John
b. the one called Mark

###### v38
a. but Paul preferred
b. him having turned away from them from Pamphylia
c. and not having gone with them to the work
d. not to take him along

###### v39
a. so (a) sharp argument took place
b. with the result that they were split up from each other
c. and then Barnabas having taken along Mark
d. sailed away to Cyprus

###### v40
a. but Paul having chosen Silas
b. he went away
c. having been entrusted to the grace of the Lord by the fellow believers

###### v41
a. and he went through Syria and Cilicia
b. strengthening the churches
